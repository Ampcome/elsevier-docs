## External Stakeholders

### Rights Holders

#### Individual Authors

| Characteristic | Engagement Approach |
|----------------|-------------------|
| **Academic researchers** | Professional, cite their work appropriately |
| **Medical professionals** | Respect clinical expertise, clear purpose |
| **Freelance creators** | Clear licensing terms, prompt payment |
| **Retired authors** | May need help with process, patience |

#### Publishers

| Publisher Type | Typical Process |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **Major scientific publishers** | Formal portals, standardized fees |
| **University presses** | Academic courtesy, reasonable fees |
| **Medical societies** | Member benefits may apply |
| **Commercial publishers** | Strict terms, higher fees |

#### Organizations

| Organization Type | Considerations |
|-------------------|---------------|
| **Government agencies** | Often public domain, verify |
| **Research institutions** | IP policies vary, verify ownership |
| **Hospitals/clinics** | Patient privacy concerns, HIPAA |
| **Foundations** | May have mission-aligned discounts |

## Building Stakeholder Relationships

### Best Practices

1. **Be Professional** - Clear, concise communication
2. **Be Persistent** - Appropriate follow-up schedule
3. **Be Respectful** - Acknowledge their time and work
4. **Be Accurate** - Double-check all details
5. **Be Documented** - Log all interactions in RPC

### Common Pain Points

| Stakeholder | Common Concern | How to Address |
|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Authors** | "Why do you need this?" | Clear explanation of use |
| **Publishers** | "Slow response" | Meet committed timelines |
| **Editorial** | "Timeline at risk" | Early communication |
| **Finance** | "Unexpected costs" | Better forecasting |
