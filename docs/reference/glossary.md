# Glossary

## A

**ACL (Annual Copyright License)**
: A blanket license from CCC that covers routine internal copying and sharing of published content within an organization.

**Adaptation**
: A modification or derivative work based on an original copyrighted work.

**Author Agreement**
: The contract between an author and publisher that specifies copyright ownership and rights transfers.

## B

**Blanket License**
: A license that covers multiple uses or items under a single agreement, typically for an annual fee.

## C

**CC (Creative Commons)**
: A nonprofit organization that provides free, standardized licenses for creators to share their work with specific permissions.

**CC-BY**
: Creative Commons Attribution license - allows reuse with attribution.

**CC-BY-NC**
: Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial - allows non-commercial reuse with attribution.

**CCC (Copyright Clearance Center)**
: A licensing organization that facilitates copyright compliance through various licensing solutions.

**Copyright**
: Legal protection for original works of authorship, giving the creator exclusive rights to use and distribute.

**Copyright Notice**
: A statement indicating copyright ownership, typically "Â© [Year] [Owner]".

**Corresponding Author**
: The author designated to handle communications about a publication, often the contact for permissions.

## D

**Derivative Work**
: A new work based on one or more existing copyrighted works.

**DOI (Digital Object Identifier)**
: A unique alphanumeric string assigned to identify digital content and provide a permanent link.

## E

**ELSA**
: Elsevier Author Platform - self-service platform for authors.

**EMSS**
: Electronic Manuscript Submission System - Elsevier's manuscript management system.

**Embargo Period**
: A period during which content cannot be freely shared, often applied to Open Access articles.

**Exclusive Rights**
: Rights granted to a single party, preventing others from the same use.

## F

**Fair Use**
: A US legal doctrine allowing limited use of copyrighted material without permission for purposes like criticism, education, or research.

**FTE (Full-Time Equivalent)**
: A unit measuring workload equal to one full-time employee.

## G

**Gold OA**
: Open Access publishing where the article is immediately freely available, typically with author-paid fees.

**Green OA**
: Open Access where authors self-archive their work in a repository, often after an embargo period.

**Grant of Rights**
: Permission given by a rights holder to use their copyrighted material.

## H

**HCM (Health Content Management)**
: Elsevier's division managing health-related content.

**HS (Health Sciences)**
: Division handling medical and healthcare-related permissions.

## I

**In-Copyright**
: A work that is still protected by copyright law.

**Intellectual Property (IP)**
: Legal rights over creations of the mind, including copyrights, patents, and trademarks.

## L

**License**
: A legal agreement granting permission to use copyrighted material under specified conditions.

**License Fee**
: Payment required to obtain permission to use copyrighted material.

## M

**Model Release**
: Written permission from a person appearing in a photo or video to use their likeness.

## N

**Non-Exclusive Rights**
: Rights that can be granted to multiple parties simultaneously.

## O

**OA (Open Access)**
: A publishing model where content is freely available to readers without subscription.

**ORCID**
: Open Researcher and Contributor ID - a unique identifier for researchers.

## P

**Permission**
: Authorization from a rights holder to use copyrighted material.

**PMID (PubMed ID)**
: A unique identifier for articles indexed in PubMed.

**PMC (PubMed Central)**
: NIH's free archive of biomedical and life sciences literature.

**Public Domain**
: Works not protected by copyright, freely available for use.

## R

**Republication**
: Using previously published content in a new publication.

**Rights Holder**
: The person or entity that owns the copyright to a work.

**RightsLink**
: CCC's online licensing platform for purchasing permissions.

**RPC (Rights & Permissions Collector)**
: Elsevier's system of record for managing permissions.

**Rule of 10**
: Policy requiring publisher contact when requesting 10+ items from the same source.

## S

**S&T (Science & Technology)**
: Division handling scientific and technical permissions.

**SHERPA/RoMEO**
: Database of publisher copyright and self-archiving policies.

**STM**
: International Association of Scientific, Technical and Medical Publishers.

**STM Guidelines**
: Framework for reasonable reuse of content between STM publishers.

**System of Record**
: The authoritative data source for a given data element (RPC for permissions).

## T

**Territory**
: Geographic scope of a license (e.g., worldwide, North America only).

**Tier**
: Classification level for permission requests based on complexity.

**Transfer of Copyright**
: When an author assigns their copyright to a publisher.

## U

**Usage Rights**
: Specific permissions for how content may be used (print, electronic, translations, etc.).

## W

**Work for Hire**
: Work created by an employee within scope of employment, with copyright owned by employer.

**World Rights**
: License granting permission for use anywhere in the world.
